# Congruity in Artificial Intelligence & Intelligent Systems

Artificial Intelligence exposes Congruity more clearly than any prior technology.
It removes biological constraints while amplifying scale, speed, and recursion.

Without Congruity, intelligence becomes unstable.

---

## Core Principle

Intelligence is not defined by capability alone.
It is defined by the proportional relationship between:

- computational power (E),
- information processing depth (I),
- system structure and constraints (S),
- functional value produced (V),
- temporal alignment (T).

When any dimension outpaces the others, intelligence degrades into risk.

## Why Alignment Alone Is Insufficient

Alignment focuses on *what* a system should do.
Congruity focuses on *whether it should scale at all*.

A perfectly aligned system can still be:
- energetically unsustainable,
- structurally brittle,
- temporally unsafe.

Alignment without Congruity is incomplete.

---

## Congruity as an Admissibility Layer

Congruity introduces a precondition:
not all possible actions are admissible.

Before execution, a system must satisfy:
- proportional energy use,
- bounded recursion,
- recoverable failure modes,
- coherent feedback loops.

Congruity does not optimize behavior.
It limits illegitimate behavior.

## Key Takeaway

More intelligence is not always better intelligence.

Intelligence that violates Congruity
eventually collapses under its own asymmetries.

Congruity is not an alignment technique.
It is the condition for intelligence to remain admissible at scale.
